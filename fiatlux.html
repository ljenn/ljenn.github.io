Fiat Lux


The Overview

Background

The iGEM Foundation is a nonprofit organization that hosts a yearly worldwide synthetic biology research conference & competition at Boston, MA - attracting 400+ teams of collegiate and graduate students.
Fiat Lux is our project submission. Our team of six from UC San Diego sought a logo that would distinguish ourselves and our project in our print materials, as we represented our school on an international platform.

  • Role: Logo Design, Visual Design
  • Duration: 10 weeks

Approach

Bioluminescence + iGEM
(light + synthetic biology)

The branding uniquely merges traditional elements of the biolouminescence project + iGEM/synthetic biology.
A lighthearted teal + professional dark navy draws parallels and associations to the concept of synthetic biology, and results in a nice balance of fun + scientific. Fiat Lux, or "Let there be light", sits on a nice coincidental overlap between the team's bioluminescence project and the university's motto.

Moodboard




Logo Ideation

  • Synthetic biology, or engineering biology, draws parallels to legos. Geometric shapes and cogs reflect the lego characteristc of engineering, or the "synthetic" side. Hexagons commonly represent nucleotides, and flasks represent labwork. Plasmids are another prospective representation of "biology".
  • The school mascot can be symbolized with the trident. The university motton is Fiat Lux, which has parallels to light, the basis of our project.
  • All final designs are drafted in black and decided on before given color.
