Treehouse

Friendship community

Project Overview

About

Treehouse is a community for people to forge friendships in a fun and organic way.

Role - Creative Director
Project Duration - January 2021 to March 2022
Responsibilities - Product design, iOS development, designing a social system, leading design sprints, high-fidelity prototyping, user interviews, usability studies, design iterations, accessible and inclusive design

Challenge

Design a solution for those who feel lonely
Today, nearly one-third of adults globally feel lonely. Loneliness can manifest into serious mental health problems such as depression, hopelessness, and suicidal tendencies - and we want to alleviate that by providing a platform for those who struggle to feel a sense of belonging elsewhere.

Goal

Design an app or website community that invites a person who feels lonely and helps them build meaningful friendships.

User Research

Surveys

We conducted a survey to gauge what users think about loneliness - data and pain points to come.

Starting the Design

    • Refine the Design - Round 1

    User research: findings

    Type: Unmoderated usability study
    Participants: 8 participants
    Length: 30 minutes

  • People are drawn to stories of others
  • People feel like doing different things at different times
  • Why would they come back?
  • Safety is a MAJOR concern
  • Issue isn't urgent for those interested, so responses are lackluster - "Yeah maybe I'll use it when I want to find concert buddies"
  • Navigation is not intuitive
  • How does this help people friends?

    • People being drawn to stories was a major point of contention, and so we decided to redesign our product to be focused on people meeting people.

  • Everyone has a clear profile where they get to talk about themselves.
  • A chat function to talk to each other before meeting.
  • Filter people by what they're doing at any given time.

    • We had the opportunity and mentorship to code this prototype, so I and another teammate coded the iOS mobile app prototype (seen in the video above) in 1 month. In the prototype, the user can: sign up, log in, fill out their profile with personal stories, scroll through a home feed of other profiles, chat with others, and filter people based off moods.

  • The prototype above placed in the final round of the Codepath Demo Day 2021 app competition. More details can be found on the Github repo here.

    • Refine the Design - Round 2

    User research: findings

    Type: Unmoderated usability study
    Participants: 4 participants
    Length: 30 minutes

  • Talking one on one with the expectation of being friends puts a lot of pressure and strain on the interaction
  • People don't know how to market themselves, so conversation dies off
  • Friendship doesn't feel as urgent as romantic relationships
  • If people don't click, then no friendship is formed
  • After too many failed tries, users lose hope in trying.
  • People do love stories still

    • Informed by the pain points, we conducted 8 user interviews and held a virtual design sprint to guide our product direction. During the design sprint, we asked "How Might We" question, identified our long-term goal, and mapped out our user journey. After ideating, we landed on 3 stellar ideas - 2 of which focused on games.

    We combined the ideas into an app of microcommunities where you can interact with people by playing many minigames.

    • Refine the Design - Round 3

    User research: findings

    Type: Unmoderated usability study, remote & recorded
    Participants: 5 participants
    Length: 1 hour

  • Like small groups
  • User is hesitant to jump right into a voice call or video call
  • User wants more information - wants to snoop and see who's in the group

    • Using the pain points above, we decided to test our idea on a real platform. We built a community with the basis of being kind and inclusive on both Reddit and Discord, growing them to over 1200 / 300 users respectively. Discord is the community that generated the most activity, and game nights are a major hit. In the Discord, we learned a lot more about our users and interacted with them more. We learned that many people are lonely because they feel unseen or unheard, and sometimes they're looking for friends because they're struggling with unique experiences - divorce, loss, disability leading to a loss of opportunity to build friendships in real life.

    After a couple of months, we built a close-knit team of moderators with a strong community. We decided to run another design sprint - I created a guide here.

    We decided on a goal, reframed problems into opportunities, and mapped out our user journey.

    We use an affinity map to categorize our "How Might We" questions.

    We spent a day sketching and brainstorming ideas and another day presenting our ideas and deciding on a solution.
    We came up with interesting ideas: 1) trial user period and 2) missions and quests in an attempt to gamify our platform and incentivize users to engage.
