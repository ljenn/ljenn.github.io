Refugee Health Alliance

Mobile app for onboarding volunteers

Project Overview

About

Refugee Health Alliance (RHA) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to providing ethical, holistic, and culturally-inclusive care and advocacy for all displaced and vulnerable populations along the US-Mexico border. RHA mobilizes a large group of volunteers, ranging from college students to licensed healthcare providers.

Role - Case Study
Project Duration - Nov 2021
Responsibilities - Conducting user interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, design iterations, accessible design


Challenge

Improving the flow of onboarding volunteers
New volunteers want to join, but the onboarding process isn't structured or intuitive to use. With the current scheduling system, the free clinic can be overstaffed or understaffed on different days.

Goal

Design a mobile app to direct the volunteer flow of RHA, which allow volunteers to sign in, read important information, sign up for events, and cancel events. This would maximize the experience and efforts of each volunteer and reduce the time spent onboarding new volunteers.

User Research

Interviews

I conducted a quick interview with 5 people using the following interview script that I created. The concept behind designing the interview questions were based off the ethnography fieldguide at Stanford's Design School, which aims to capture stories and emotions from people.

I asked volunteers to map out a user-generated journey map (one person's user journey map is pictured below). Afterwards, I built empathy maps to better understand and assess the needs of the group I'm designing for. One main user group identified were college students from Southern California colleges (ie. UCLA, UC San Diego, Clairemont Colleges, others). The research showed us that it was hard for volunteers to find availabilities, and volunteers often wouldn't cancel if they couldn't go.

Information Architecture

Site Map of App

I split the flow into 3 main categories:

  • The Pre-screen - or the onboarding: read important information and upload necessary documents or qualifications
  • Volunteer Events - show information on upcoming events
  • Scheduling - allows volunteers to sign up or cancel their attendance for an event





    • Starting the Design

    Paper wireframes

  • I took the stacked events from the bottom three-fourths of the first screen. This design allows users to easily see all upcoming events.
  • We wanted to start this out as minimalistic as possible, so I omitted the calendar.

    • Digital wireframes

  • Users sometimes found it confusing what the volunteer events were, so I organized the layout in a way that delivered information as directly and simply as possible.
  • It was important for users to understand all the steps needed to sign up and volunteer.

    • Low-fidelity prototype

    I created a low-fidelity prototype. The flow let the user sign up, onboard, and schedule for a volunteer event.



    Refine the Design

    Usability study: findings

    Type: Unmoderated usability study
    Participants: 6 participants
    Length: 30 minutes

    I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.


    Lower Priority Findings (Future Steps)

  • There are only 3-4 pages, so it would be better to change the hamburger menu to a botton navigation bar.
  • Something that users indicated wantign was a visual calendar because many people visualized the week in a calendar format. Many people struggled to remember what day it was today, so seeing all the upcoming events in a scrollable page felt disorganizing.
  • Accessibility consideration - Use animation and interaction details to nudge users towards certain parts of the process.

    • Mockups

  • It was convenient to show all the possible onboarding materials in one page, but users who needed to onboard first benefited from some guidance through a button that directed newcomers to the onboarding page.
  • The usability study showed that communication in between volunteers was needed, especially for newcomers who might have questions and obstacles finding the new location.

    • I integrated a chat feature into the app because volunteers reported:

  • Many volunteers are new, and they frequently run into issues or have questions - but they don't know who to ask.
  • Everyone is usually directed to a Whatsapp group chat, but their personal phone number is revealed to everyone in the same group, which many don't want.
  • Volunteers cross the border together for safety. If someone is running late, then having a way to stay connected is not just convenient but important.

    • High Fidelity Prototype

    The high fidelity prototype let users sign in, onboard, schedule for volunteer events, and also chat with other volunteers.
