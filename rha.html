Refugee Health Alliance

Mobile app for volunteer onboarding

Project Overview

About

Refugee Health Alliance is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to providing ethical, holistic, and culturally-inclusive care and advocacy for all displaced and vulnerable populations along the US-Mexico border.

Role - Case Study
Project Duration - Nov 2021
Responsibilities - Visual branding, Wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, design iterations, usability interviews, responsive design

Challenge

Improving the flow of onboarding volunteers
Refugee Health Alliance (RHA) currently offers healthcare services for people in multiple areas within the Baja California region. To carry out their efforts, they mobilize a large group of volunteers, ranging from college students to licensed healthcare providers. There are always new volunteers to onboard, and there is no structured process. Additionally, there is no set sign up process, so the free clinic can be overstaffed or understaffed on different days.

Goal

A mobile app that allows volunteers to complete their onboarding process, sign up for events, as well as cancel events would maximize the experience and efforts that every volunteer puts in, and it would also reduce the time spent onboarding new volunteers.

User Research

Pain points

  • Too many Whatsapp groups - Volunteers on the same day are put into a massive Whatsapp group. Hundreds of Whatsapp groups are created each week.
  • Unsure who's leading the groups - There are multiple leaders, and it changes every week.
  • Confused what to expect - Volunteers are never sure what exactly their responsibilities are.
  • Want flexibility in scheduling - Many volunteers are licensed providers or college students, both with busy and changing schedules. Volunteers currently email to confirm or cancel attendance, which can be difficult to keep track.

    Testing / Design Iterations

    Usability Interview Findings

    Type: Unmoderated usability study
    Participants: 6 participants
    Length: 30 minutes

    I integrated a chat feature into the app because volunteers reported:

  • Many volunteers are new, and they frequently run into issues or have questions - but they don't know who to ask.
  • Everyone is usually directed to a Whatsapp group chat, but their personal phone number is revealed to everyone in the same group, which many don't want.
  • Volunteers cross the border together for safety. If someone is running late, then having a way to stay connected is not just convenient but important.
