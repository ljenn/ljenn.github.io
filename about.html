The Backstory


I want to help people, so I studied Biology in college. At the time, I believed that many of the solutions to our generation's most urgent issues were rooted and influenced by science. Halfway through, I switched into Bioinformatics because I recognized that our future is with data.

Mentored by inspiring and brilliant scientists, I studied novel molecules and fine tuned machine learning models through a neverending process of trial and error. Undergraduate pipetting segued into post-graduate full-time coding into... a feeling that something was off. I kind of wanted to impact people more directly.

To scratch at the unshakable feeling, I began exploring, volunteering, and traveling. From Macau to Finland to Mexico, I interacted and worked with diverse human beings - and they tuned me into the unique cultural nuances and universal emotions we all felt. When we walk together, many of our differences are united by what simmers down to empathy. It repainted our realities on a global scale down to the individual, and it brings happiness, demands inclusion, and changes stories - at least, it changed mine.

The world becomes a better place when we're intentional about considering others, and user experience addresses the issues I care about far more broadly and tangibly than any biology experiment.

That is why I'm here.
