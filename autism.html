Autism Simplified

Helping me and others better understand the diversity of autism.

Autism Simplified

  • Oct - Dec 2017
  • Psychology
  • Education

'Autism Simplified', now integrated as a sub-section of my personal website, was a website developed during my college Public Rhetoric course. The articles are written for the neurotypical, and the purpose of this project is to build understanding around those who are autistic.

In speaking about autism, I strive to tap into the heart public rhetoric and leverage the English language in a way that avoids the use of negative connotations. Sometimes, the language we use can unintentionally dehumanize marginalized and misrepresented groups. Autism is an association that's coupled with a lot of bias. My goal is to explain the diversity of autism in a light that provides more understanding and empathy. I hope to showcase them as another human being - no less than the rest of us - only born with a unique set of individual differences.

I acknowledge that it's sometimes offensive or frowned upon for a neurotypical person to explain autism, especially so when many autistic people can speak for themselves. However, a large part of this project also serves to improve my own understanding. This is a platform for me to share my findings with others, and I am always open to constructive feedback.
