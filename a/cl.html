1. Resilience in high pressure work environments

In college, I competed in a global synthetic biology competition where our team was sponsored with over $20,000+ funds by biotechnology companies. Our experiments failed, and we flew out to Boston to deliver a project with no results.

As a freshman with no experience, I applied to a research team of veterans. In 4 months, we designed a research project, fundraised for money, executed experiments, and presented our results. It was a huge time investment: 50-60 workhours per week, sometimes spending over 20+ hours in the lab in a single day. The yearly retention rate was 7%, or one person. After our lack of results, I was the only person who stayed from my year.

I had a vision, and for the next 3 years, I co-founded and built synbio. from ground up - a synthetic biology project incubator. I was the youngest and most inexperienced in a room full of upperclassmen and Master's/PhD researchers, but I persevered in the face of my insecurities. I recruited new members who we mentored and eventually placed second worldwide out of over 400 teams. Member retention tripled and growth doubled. synbio. began as 'failures,' but when I graduated, we had accomplishments to our name.

3. Why design

When I considered a career in medicine, I imagined a company with no overhead work for providers, a tri-fusion system integrating medical, dental, and mental health, and a normalized culture of not having all the medical answers - transformed into a department for undiagnosed diseases.

I love design because it lets me reimagine systems. Imagine a better electronic medical records system. Imagine an education system where feedback is the focus - instead of red marks on an exam, kids are given immediate corrective feedback while learning, which teaches them how to learn. Imagine a world where education is taught through life experiences and mixed learning styles, and subjects are made accessible to English Second Language speakers and those neurodivergent or underresourced. Imagine a world that celebrates culture, that advocates financial literacy and emotional health, and that leverages everyone's unique strengths. Those are all the things that I've been involved in, and the web3 space draws me in because it's a system that lets us reimagine systems.

I also love design because I love people, and that's why I volunteer. Sometimes, no matter how complex the root cause, the feeling of resonation is all anyone ever wants.
