Anise Health Client Portal

The Challenge: For their pilot program, Anise Health needed a user web portal that allows for a smooth onboarding process and user journey from registration to payment & scheduling.

The Overview

Background

I was the full design team at Anise Health. Anise Health is a culturally informed mental health telehealth platform for the Asian American community. They are an early stage startup founded by two Harvard Business graduates. Prior to this project, I contributed visual content as a part of their marketing team, growing their Instagram account from zero followers.

  • Role: UX Research, UI + UX Designer, Visual Designer
  • Duration: 8 weeks

Responsibilities

• Wireframed all pages of client portal
• Rolled out front-end specifications for engineers to implement
• Usability interviews
• Bridged their brand and user experience across their client portal + marketing campaigns

Deliverables

A user-centered mobile-friendly pilot product for the client from forms to emails to the client portal.

Design

Minimize gender biases in mental health with visual design

Statistics reveal that there are more female therapists and clients than male ones. Our choice in pictures, colors, and visual styles need to strike a balance between normative masculine and feminine associations because we want to tell the user that we're for all genders - binary or not.

Interweave science to battle stigma existing in Asian cultures

Asian cultures have a long-standing stigma towards mental health, which results in high percentages of Asians who need yet do not go to therapy. Our choice of words and content needs to be scientific and objective to build matter-of-fact credibility for anyone struggling with the validity of therapy. This is also reflected in our presentation - choosing to chart data, display graphs, etc.

Personalization & Diversity

Given the diverse ethnicties within the Asian demographic plus the intimate nature of everyone's personal life, choosing the right therapist is an important and personal decision to the client. Information should be organized in a way where the client feels that they're making the most informed and best decision for themselves.

Clear, friendly, easy on the mind

Clutter causes unnecessary cognitive load, so we focused on a clean and simple design - no extra details to think about. The rounded corners and choice of colors in visual design provides a gentle feel and peace of mind. The site needs to be visually welcoming - warm, simple, and empowering. We want to make it easy for people to say: Yes! Let's try it.

Strike a balance between technical debt and fast implementation

My background in computer science is advantageous in helping me choose the most optimal and time-feasible designs for our team of engineers to implement. For the pilot launch, all clients are aware of the company's budding stage, so it's a unique situation where Anise's priority leaned towards functionality.
