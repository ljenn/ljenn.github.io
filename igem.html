UC San Diego iGEM


The Challenge Our iGEM team needed a brand design that would fit our project and make us memorable in a competition of 400+ teams from across the world.

The Overview

Background

The iGEM Foundation is a nonprofit organization that hosts an annual worldwide synthetic biology research competition. High school, college, and graduate students fundraise and execute a research project within one summer, then fly out to Boston to present their findings at an international conference. Our team of six at UC San Diego received sponsorship from local biotechnology companies, and we wanted to design a logo for better outreach and publicity.

Deliverables

The UC San Diego iGEM team has a logo unique to our project, tshirt and flyer designs, and a website to document our research project.

  • Role: Logo Designs, UX, Visual Design
  • Duration 10 weeks

Inspiration

Design Goals

  • Caters to multiple audiences. Professional enough (no cartoons), yet inviting and easy to understand for non-researchers and the general public.
  • Logo. Reflects what our project is about.
  • T-shirt. Emphasize the school we're from at a conference of 400+ schools. No logo - the majority vote did not want the logo on our shirt. The professors and post doctorates especially wanted a more neutral shirt they could wear long after the competition was over.

Moodboard




Logo Design

  • Geometric shapes and cog gears reflect the lego-like characteristc of synthetic biology. Cutting the cog gear midway would represent the concept of plasmids.
  • Flasks represent labwork. Pentagons and hexagons are also commonly used to represent nucleotides in biology.
  • Light is used to represent our project and our school's Fiat Lux motto. Other symbols like the trident were considered.
  • The final logo is confirmed in black before given color.
